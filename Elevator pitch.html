My Personal Elevator Pitch

I am DUSHIME Simbi Benie, a prospective artist and digital art developer. I am a hardworking student who recently graduated high school in history, economics, and geography. I was ranked first in my class consistently.

During my high school journey, I maintained academic excellence and served in various leadership roles. I was the chairperson of the school debate club, secretary of the writing club, and secretary of the student council. In addition, I was a member of the business club.

I attended and won various debate tournaments hosted by iDebate Rwanda, an NGO aiming at improving the critical thinking and communication skills of high school students. I also participated in writing contests such as the Queen’s Commonwealth Essay Competition, and I never hesitated to grow and share my artistic skills.

Upon high school completion, I signed up to work with iDebate Rwanda as a volunteer to help build critical thinking among students. I also temporarily worked as a cashier in Afya Dairy, where I filled financial statements, managed inventory, and corrected errors in financial statements.

In addition, I dedicated my gap year to art and programming, eager to learn how to create apps and software. I majorly learned HTML, CSS, C++, and JavaScript.

So far, I am working on SA Studio (Simbi Artz Studio), a digital art tool that consists of drawing and painting tools, comic creation tools, as well as other art tools that are easy to navigate and bring out fun in art.

In addition, I worked hand in hand with Titan Minds in the phase 2 of ALx pathway program, to create AgriRwanda, a digital platform that connects Rwandan farmers and enlightens them with essential skills, weather patterns, etc.

As I reflect on my journey, I have acquired several skills, such as public speaking, programming fundamentals, writing skills, Microsoft Office skills, etc., and I am determined to use my skills to be an impactful artist and digital art developer.
